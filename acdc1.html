Modulation Techniques

ASK/BASK

Amplitude Shift Keying represents digital data as variations in the amplitude of a carrier wave. Binary 1 is represented by a carrier wave at constant amplitude, and binary 0 by no carrier wave.

s(t) = A(t) · cos(2πfct)

FSK/BFSK

Frequency Shift Keying represents digital data as changes in the frequency of a carrier wave. Binary 1 is represented by one frequency, and binary 0 by a different frequency.

s(t) = A · cos(2πfbitt)

PSK/BPSK

Phase Shift Keying represents digital data as changes in the phase of a carrier wave. Binary digits are represented by shifting the phase of the carrier wave by 180 degrees.

s(t) = A · cos(2πfct + φbit)

About This Simulator

This enhanced simulator provides clear visualization of digital modulation techniques. The comparative signal views allow for detailed analysis of how digital signals modulate carrier waves.

Educational Value

By comparing signals through vertically stacked views, students can clearly see the relationship between digital inputs, carrier waves, and modulated outputs.
